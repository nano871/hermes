## 3. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS
Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship. Contractor is solely responsible for:
(a) its own work hours, methods, and location;
(b) providing its own equipment, tools, and materials;
(c) obtaining any licenses or permits required for the Services;
(d) its own benefits, insurance, and workers' compensation.

**⚠️ MISCLASSIFICATION RISK:** Minnesota applies a multi-factor test for independent contractor status. Key factors favoring IC classification:
- Contractor controls how work is performed (not Company)
- Contractor provides own tools/equipment
- Contractor can work for other clients
- Contractor is not integrated into Company's daily operations
- Payment is by project or invoice (not salary)

If these factors are not met, Contractor may be reclassified as an employee by MN DLI or IRS, resulting in liability for back taxes, penalties, and benefits. **Principal should verify the relationship satisfies these factors before engaging.**

## 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 4.1 Work Product
All work product created by Contractor specifically for Company under this Agreement (the **"Work Product"**) shall be owned by Company. Contractor hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in the Work Product to Company.

### 4.2 Pre-Existing Materials
Contractor retains ownership of all pre-existing tools, libraries, code, methodologies, and materials (**"Pre-Existing Materials"**). Contractor grants Company a perpetual, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to use Pre-Existing Materials incorporated into the Work Product solely as part of the Work Product.

### 4.3 AI-Generated Work
If Contractor uses AI tools in creating Work Product:
(a) Contractor shall disclose AI use to Company;
(b) Contractor represents that AI-generated components comply with this Agreement's specifications;
(c) Contractor acknowledges that AI-generated outputs may not be copyrightable under *Thaler v. Perlmutter*;
(d) Contractor shall maintain records of human creative input for any deliverables requiring copyright protection.

### 4.4 Moral Rights
To the extent permitted by law, Contractor waives any moral rights in the Work Product.
