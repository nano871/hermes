# SAFFRON DESIGNS — CLIENT RECORDS RETENTION POLICY

**Purpose:** How long to keep client files, contracts, and data.
**Compliance:** MN contract statute of limitations (6 years), IRS recordkeeping (3-7 years), MCDPA/CCPA data minimization requirements.

---

## RETENTION SCHEDULE

| Record Type | Retention Period | Disposal Method | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Signed contracts & SOWs** | 7 years after project completion | Permanent (digital archive) | MN written contract SOL = 6 years + buffer. Also: tax records, potential disputes. |
| **Invoices & payment records** | 7 years | Secure deletion | IRS requires 3-7 years for business records. MN SOL for contract claims = 6 years. |
| **Client correspondence (email)** | 3 years after project completion | Secure deletion | Covers warranty period + reasonable buffer. |
| **Client design files (PSD, AI, Figma)** | 1 year after project completion | Secure deletion | Warranty period (30-90 days) + reasonable grace. Client should have their own copies. |
| **Client code/source files** | 1 year after project completion | Secure deletion | Client received their copy at handoff. Retain only if maintenance retainer is active. |
| **Client credentials (passwords)** | Deleted at project handoff | Immediate deletion after delivery | Security best practice. Client must change passwords anyway. |
| **Client data in active systems (CMS, hosting)** | Duration of contract + 90 days | Automated deletion | Per Web Design Agreement §[X], data retained 90 days post-termination. |
| **Maintenance retainer records** | 3 years after retainer ends | Secure deletion | Covers billing disputes. |
| **Tax records (1099-NEC, receipts)** | 7 years | Permanent (digital archive) | IRS requirement. |

## DELETION PROCEDURE

### For Digital Files
1. Move files to a "Pending Deletion" folder with deletion date in filename
2. On deletion date: permanently delete (bypass Recycle Bin)
3. If using cloud storage: check "permanently delete" (not just trash)
4. Document deletion in a simple log: "Client [Name] — files deleted [Date]"

### For Physical Files
1. Shred documents (cross-cut shredder, not strip-cut)
2. If no shredder available: burn or use professional shredding service

### For Email
1. Archive emails older than retention period to a separate archive folder
2. No need to delete archived emails — just move out of active inbox

## ANNUAL CLEANUP

Schedule a **[once per year]** cleanup (recommended: January, after tax season):

- [ ] Identify all completed projects from [N] years ago
- [ ] Verify retention period has expired
- [ ] Confirm client is not on a maintenance retainer
- [ ] Delete files per the schedule above
- [ ] Log deletions for recordkeeping

## DISCLAIMER
This policy does not override legal holds or litigation preservation obligations.
If you receive a subpoena or litigation hold notice, retain all relevant records
until the hold is released regardless of this policy.
