# SAFFRON DESIGNS — BUSINESS CONTINUITY PLAN

**Purpose:** What happens if you are unavailable (emergency, illness, travel).
**Keep a printed copy** with your emergency contacts.

---

## TIER 1: SHORT ABSENCE (1-5 DAYS)

### Before Any Absence
• Set email autoresponder: "I'm currently unreachable. For urgent website issues, contact [emergency contact]: [phone]."
• Give emergency contact access to: [password manager name] (with client credentials), hosting provider logins, domain registrar logins
• Notify clients with active projects of the absence dates

### Emergency Contact
| Name | Role | Phone | Email | Access To |
|------|------|-------|-------|-----------|
| [Name] | [Developer / colleague] | [Phone] | [Email] | Password manager, hosting, domain |

### Critical Operations (Covered by Emergency Contact)
• Site-down response: restore from backup, contact hosting provider
• Domain expiration: renew domain (use business credit card)
• Client urgent requests: triage and communicate

## TIER 2: EXTENDED ABSENCE (1-4 WEEKS)

All of Tier 1, plus:
• Grant emergency contact write access to business bank account (for payments, domain renewals, hosting)
• Share client project status document with emergency contact
• Forward business phone to emergency contact
• Set up a simple "Client Emergency" form/email that goes to both you and emergency contact

## TIER 3: LONG-TERM OUTAGE

All of Tier 1 + 2, plus:
• Emergency contact should notify all active maintenance clients within 5 business days
• Provide clients with instructions to access their hosting/domain credentials (stored in password manager)
• Provide clients with backup files (stored in [location])
• Assist clients with transitioning to a new provider if needed
