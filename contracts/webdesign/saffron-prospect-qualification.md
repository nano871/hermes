# SAFFRON DESIGNS — PROSPECT QUALIFICATION FRAMEWORK

**Purpose:** Evaluate potential clients BEFORE sending a proposal.
**Rule:** It's better to decline the wrong client than to take on a nightmare.

---

## SCREENING QUESTIONS

Ask these on the discovery call or intake questionnaire:

| Question | Green Flag | Yellow Flag | 🔴 Red Flag |
|----------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| "What's your budget range?" | $[N]+ or "budget is flexible" | "I don't know — what do you think?" | "As cheap as possible" / "My nephew could do it" |
| "What's your timeline?" | 4-12 weeks | "ASAP" with reasonable expectations | "I need it yesterday" / "Next week" |
| "Who makes the decisions?" | One person with authority | A committee of 2-3 people | "My cousin/my spouse/my board needs to approve" |
| "Do you have content ready?" | Yes, or willing to hire a copywriter | "I'll get to it" (vague) | "I thought you'd write it" / "I'll know it when I see it" |
| "What happens if we don't work together?" | Will find another designer | Might DIY it | Will do nothing / "I'll just use Wix" |
| "Have you worked with a designer before?" | Yes, understands the process | First time — needs education | Had a bad experience and blames the designer |
| "Can you provide examples of websites you like?" | Provides 2-3 examples with specific feedback | "I just want something modern" | "Make it look like Apple/Amazon/Tesla" |

## SCORING

| Score | Assessment | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **7/7 green** | Ideal client | Send proposal |
| **5-6/7 green** | Good prospect | Send proposal with clear scope guardrails |
| **3-4/7 green** | Marginal | Proceed with caution. Higher deposit (60%). Tight scope. |
| **0-2/7 green** | High risk | Decline politely or price significantly higher |

## DECLINE TEMPLATE

> Thank you for considering Saffron Designs for your project. After reviewing your requirements, I don't believe this project is the best fit for my current workload and expertise.
>
> I'd recommend checking out [alternative resource: e.g., Fiverr/Upwork for budget projects, or specific agency for complex needs].
>
> I wish you the best with your project.

*Note: This is more professional than "sorry, not interested." Keep bridges intact — they may come back with a better budget next year.*
