Re: Should "Saffron Designs" be federally registered as a trademark?

WHAT A TRADEMARK DOES
A federally registered trademark (®) gives you the exclusive right to use
the mark nationwide in connection with your services. It:
• Prevents others from using a confusingly similar name in your industry
• Gives you the right to sue in federal court for infringement
• Creates a public record of your ownership
• Allows you to use the ® symbol (vs. ™ which requires no registration)
• Can be licensed or sold as an asset

ASSESSMENT

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Is "Saffron Designs" distinctive? | Moderate. "Saffron" is arbitrary (good). "Designs" is descriptive (weak). Combined, it may qualify as "suggestive" — protectable but not the strongest mark. |
| Is anyone else using it? | Unknown — needs USPTO database search. Similar names likely exist. |
| Is it worth registering? | For a solo operation serving local clients? Probably not yet. For a growing agency with national aspirations? Yes. |

RECOMMENDATION

RIGHT NOW: Use ™ (unregistered trademark) on your website and materials.
This gives you common-law rights based on use. It costs nothing and signals
ownership. "Saffron Designs™"

SOON (if budget allows): Conduct a basic USPTO clearance search to confirm
the mark is available. If clear, file a federal application. The ® symbol
adds credibility and legal protection.

LATER (if business grows): Federally register once revenue exceeds $100K/yr
or if you encounter a name conflict. Also consider registering "Saffron
Automations" for AI consulting services (separate class).
